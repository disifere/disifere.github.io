## General description

This study examines how the use of a wearable device to monitor physical activity goals affects change in- the activity levels, sedentary behaviour & total wellness.


### What was done 
The intervention group (27 +-4) was given a wearable activity monitoring device that tracks time spent in 'sedentary, lite, moderate & vigourous-intensity physical activity'.
Users were then provided direct access to the collected data through visualisation. Users were also given the option of Goal-setting, with the wearable reflecting the daily progress.

The study ran over a 4-week period, with the different activity levels as well as a wellness evaluation where measured.

### What were the main results
Sedentary behaviour decreased, and for all 3 physical activities saw meaningful increases. The wellness score of the intervention group also showed a meaningful increase.

This transaltes to all measures, when compared to the control group, showing a statistically significant difference with a p < 0.01, and all but 'vigorous-physical activity'(d = 0.67- Moderate) showing large effect sizes (Cohen d > 1).

### Conclusion
Physical activity is good for you, and having some monitoring of sort helps with providing some motivation to reduce sedentary behaviours. Given the relatively short time frame of 4-weeks, caution is advised when extrapolating to a longer period of time. However, these are promising results.

## Why is it special to you?

This study is special to me as I can anecdotally confirm these results. During my bachelors, I gained ownership over a smart watch and beyond the typical uses of a smartwatch, notifications and media control, I also used it for activity monitoring. At least for me, over the approximately 1 year that I used it, I did see a meaningful increase in my activity levels. Due to privacy concerns I have stopped using health/activity tracking applications or devices (and have these settings turned off on my phone), and I have noticed that I my lifestyle has become more sedentary again. There are various reasons for this, but the thing that having a smartwatch did best, was remind me to be active.

## Why is it a good example to you?

This study is a good example as it clearly highlights effectiveness that a wearable based intervention can have on ones lifestyle & behaviour.
Despite it's shortcomings of using a subjective measure, the self-report measures used in the study are validated measures. At the risk of false equivalency- Depression reports too are often self-reports.
